# Mission Brief

## Overall Objective
Create super practical wisdom document translating statistical inference concepts into actionable principles for everyday thinking and problem-solving (from daily decisions to serious challenges).

## Success Criteria
- **Actionable:** Each principle has clear "do this, not that" guidance
- **Pattern + Validation framework:** Explicitly addresses how we see patterns and how to validate them
- **Grounded in Chapter 1:** Maps directly to statistical concepts (sampling, bias, variance, CLT, inference)
- **No fluff:** Concise, precise, immediately useful
- **Broad applicability:** Works for day-to-day decisions AND complex problems

## Specialists Involved

### 1. Pattern Breaker (lateral-thinking)
**Why:** Generate practical frameworks from statistical concepts, break down "better thinking" systematically
**Delivers:** Practical wisdom document with actionable principles

### 2. Logic Validator (vertical-thinking)
**Why:** Stress-test each principle for logical soundness, eliminate platitudes
**Delivers:** Validated principles that hold up to scrutiny

## Strategic Context

**Source Material:**
- Chapter 1 notebook: populations vs samples, parameters vs statistics, bias-variance, CLT, three types of inference
- cognitive_takeaways.md: Our cognitive bugs (confuse SD/SE, poor sampling intuition, see patterns in noise)

**Key Insight to Leverage:**
Statistical thinking = systematic correction of cognitive bugs. The same framework applies to everyday reasoning:
1. We see patterns (natural human tendency)
2. We need validation (statistical rigor)
3. We confuse signal and noise
4. We underestimate uncertainty
5. We trust small "samples" (limited experiences)

**Constraints:**
- Must be practical, not academic
- Must work outside statistics (business decisions, personal choices, complex problems)
- Must be memorable and actionable

## Collaboration Pattern
**Sequential Pipeline:**
1. Pattern Breaker creates practical wisdom framework
2. Logic Validator validates logical soundness
